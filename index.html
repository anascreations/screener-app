∿ MA Strategy — 7-Filter System Overview
The MA (Moving Average) strategy identifies high-probability momentum entries by stacking multiple confirmations. Never enter on less than 5 of 7 filters passing. The more filters aligned, the higher your win probability.
01
F1 — Price above MA5 (Immediate Momentum)
The market price must be above the 5-period MA. This is your fastest momentum filter. If price breaks below MA5, momentum has stalled — exit immediately or reduce position. The closer price is to MA5 (within 1%), the better the entry quality.
✅ Pass: Price > MA5  |  🔴 Fail: Price ≤ MA5 → Skip trade
02
F2 — MA5 above MA20 (Short-Term Trend)
MA5 crossing above MA20 is a "Golden Cross" on the short-term. This confirms that recent momentum is stronger than the 20-day average. The gap between MA5 and MA20 shows trend acceleration — a widening gap is good but >5% means it may reverse soon.
✅ Pass: MA5 > MA20  |  🔴 Fail: MA5 ≤ MA20 → Do not enter
03
F3 — MA20 above MA50 (Medium-Term Trend)
This confirms the intermediate trend direction. MA20 > MA50 means the medium-term trajectory is bullish. The percentage gap reveals trend maturity: 1–3% = fresh trend (S++ grade), 3–5% = healthy (A grade), 5–8% = maturing (B), >10% = exhausted (X grade, reduce or skip).
✅ Pass: MA20 > MA50  |  🔴 Fail → Wait for alignment
04
F4 — MA200 Macro Filter (Optional but Powerful)
The 200-day MA is the institutional benchmark. Funds buy when price is above MA200 — this is your macro tailwind. Trading above MA200 = swimming with the tide. Below MA200 = fighting institutions. Skip this filter only if your timeframe is very short-term.
✅ Ideal: Price > MA200  |  ⚠️ Optional: adds +8 weight to score
05
F5 — KDJ Oscillator (Timing Precision)
KDJ identifies overbought/oversold conditions and momentum direction. K>D means fast momentum is above slow momentum — bullish. J<20 = oversold bounce opportunity. J>80 = overbought, reduce size by 50%. J>90 = extreme, skip. Use KDJ to time your exact entry candle within an otherwise valid setup.
✅ Best: K>D, J 20–80  |  ⚠️ Reduce: J>80  |  🔴 Skip: K<D
06
F6 — MACD (DIF & DEA Crossover Momentum)
MACD confirms trend momentum strength. DIF>DEA means the fast EMA is accelerating above the slow EMA — bullish. Both above zero line = strongest signal (Strong Bull). DIF ≈ DEA = imminent cross, watch but wait for confirmation. Histogram expanding = momentum building. Histogram shrinking = momentum fading, tighten stop.
🚀 Best: DIF>DEA>0  |  ✅ Good: DIF>DEA  |  🔴 Skip: DIF<DEA
07
F7 — Volume Ratio + ADX (Conviction Confirmation)
Volume Ratio >1.2 means today's volume exceeds average — smart money is participating. Never trade breakouts on low volume (<0.8×). ADX measures trend strength regardless of direction: ADX >28 = valid trend, ADX >50 = momentum surge. Low ADX (<20) = ranging market — MAs become unreliable. Require BOTH volume and ADX for a high-conviction setup.
✅ Vol>1.2× + ADX>28  |  🟡 Vol 0.8-1.2 = caution  |  🔴 ADX<20 = skip

≋ EMA Strategy — Why EMA Over MA?
EMA (Exponential Moving Average) gives more weight to recent price action, making it faster and more responsive than simple MA. Professional traders prefer EMA for momentum stocks and volatile instruments. The EMA 8/21/55 stack is the industry-standard momentum framework used by hedge funds.
01
F1 — Price above EMA8 (Live Momentum Pulse)
EMA8 is the heartbeat of momentum trading. Professional traders will not hold a position if price closes below EMA8. The distance above EMA8 tells you timing: 0–2% = ideal entry, 2–5% = acceptable, 5–10% = stretched (wait for pullback to EMA8), >10% = overextended (skip, wait for pullback). On a valid setup, enter when price dips close to EMA8 and bounces on lower volume.
🎯 Ideal: 0–2% above EMA8  |  ⚠️ Stretched: >5% → Wait for pullback
02
F2 — EMA8 above EMA21 (Short-Term Bull Trend)
This is the momentum alignment filter. EMA8 > EMA21 means the 8-day velocity is outpacing the 21-day. This is the minimum condition for an uptrend. The gap percentage tells you phase: 0–1% = just crossed (highest probability zone — fresh breakout), 1–3% = building momentum, >5% = maturing. A gap narrowing from above is a warning sign.
✅ Pass: EMA8 > EMA21  |  🚀 Best entry: just crossed (0–2% gap)
03
F3 — EMA21 above EMA55 (Trend Maturity & Grade)
This is the trend maturity indicator. Use the Grade system: S++ (1–3% gap) = fresh institutional trend just starting — best risk/reward. A grade (3–5%) = healthy trend with room. B (5–8%) = maturing, consider smaller size. C (8–10%) = late stage, use tight trailing stop. X (>10%) = exhausted, do not initiate new longs. When buying a B or C grade, you need a stronger pullback to EMA8 for entry.
🚀 S++ Best  |  ✅ A Good  |  ⚠️ B–C Reduce size  |  🛑 X Skip
04
F4 — EMA200 Institutional Macro Filter
The 200 EMA is where large funds rebalance. Price above EMA200 = institutional uptrend. Below EMA200 = institutional downtrend. Even if EMA8/21/55 are bullish, fighting the EMA200 trend lowers your win rate significantly. For swing trades, always require price > EMA200. For very short-term momentum scalps, this filter can be reduced in weight.
✅ Strong: Price > EMA200  |  ⚠️ Caution: Price below EMA200
05
ADX + Ichimoku + VWAP — Confluence Boosters
ADX: Trend strength engine. >28 = strong directional move. <20 = ranging market where EMAs give false signals. Always check ADX before entering on EMA alone.

Ichimoku Cloud: Price above the cloud = strong bullish confluence. Inside cloud = indecision — wait. Below cloud = bearish — do not buy.

VWAP: The institutional average price of the day. Price above VWAP = intraday bulls in control. Use as an intraday entry trigger: buy the first touch of VWAP from above on a day where all EMAs are bullish.
Best combo: ADX>28 + Price above Ichimoku cloud + Price above VWAP = Very high conviction
≋ EMA Momentum Trading Playbook
🚀 Setup A — Fresh Breakout
EMA8 just crossed above EMA21 (F2 gap 0–2%), price 0–2% above EMA8, ADX just rising above 25, MACD histogram turning positive. Highest win rate setup. Full position. Tight stop at 1× ATR below EMA8.
📈 Setup B — EMA8 Pullback
Trend established (EMA8>21>55), price pulled back to touch EMA8 on low volume, bouncing with KDJ oversold recovery (J rising from <30). Best risk/reward. Full position. SL = EMA8 − ATR×1.5.
⚠️ Setup C — Momentum Chase
All EMAs aligned but price is 5–10% above EMA8. High-risk. If ADX >40 and volume surging, can enter 25–50% size only. Mandatory stop at ATR×1.5. Watch for any candle close below EMA8 — exit immediately.
🔴 Do Not Trade
EMA21 below EMA55, price below EMA8, ADX <20 (ranging), KDJ K<D, MACD DIF<DEA, or price below EMA200 in a bear market. These setups have <40% win rate.
◈ Gold XAUUSD — Professional Trading Framework
Gold is the world's most-traded safe-haven commodity. It has unique drivers: USD strength (inverse), geopolitical risk, real interest rates, and central bank demand. Never trade gold without checking the DXY direction first. Gold moves in wider ATR swings than stocks — adjust position size accordingly.
💵
DXY Correlation
Gold and the US Dollar Index move inversely ~80% of the time. DXY falling = Gold bullish. DXY rising = Gold bearish. Always check DXY before entering gold. A falling DXY adds 5 points to your score — it's a significant tailwind.
📊
RSI Sweet Spot
Gold's ideal RSI14 entry zone is 50–70. Below 50 = momentum not confirmed. Above 70 = overbought (reduce size). Above 80 = extreme — only hold existing positions, do not add. The 50 line is the most important for gold — it separates bull trend from weakness.
⏰
Session Timing
London-NY Overlap (13:00–17:00 UTC) = PRIME. This 4-hour window accounts for ~60% of daily gold volume. Best breakouts happen here. London open (08:00) = second best. Avoid entering new positions in Tokyo/Sydney sessions — spreads widen and moves are thin.
📐
Fibonacci Levels
Gold respects Fibonacci levels exceptionally well due to algorithmic trading. 38.2% and 61.8% are the key support levels in a bull trend pullback. Enter at 38.2% if trend is strong. Enter at 61.8% if you missed the 38.2%. A close below 78.6% warns trend may be reversing.
🏦
EMA200 Institutional
Major funds buy gold above EMA200 and sell below it. The EMA200 acts as strong dynamic support. Do not initiate new gold longs below EMA200. In a bull market, the EMA200 is the deepest acceptable pullback level — a touch of EMA200 with RSI <35 is the highest risk/reward long entry.
⚡
ADX for Gold
Gold needs ADX >28 for reliable EMA signals. In a <20 ADX environment, gold ranges between support and resistance — use range trading instead of momentum. ADX >45 in gold = powerful momentum surge, common during geopolitical events. Use these surges for momentum longs, but tighten stop immediately after.
◈ Gold 7-Filter Entry Protocol
01
Macro Check — EMA200 + DXY
Before anything else: Is XAUUSD above EMA200? Is DXY falling or flat? If NO to both = do not trade long today. If yes to both = proceed to session check. This takes 30 seconds but saves enormous losses from counter-trend trades.
02
Session Quality — Are you in London or NY hours?
Check the session badge in the top right. London-NY overlap (13:00–17:00 UTC) = green light. Outside prime hours = reduce size by 50% or wait. Thin sessions create whipsaw moves that trigger stops unfairly.
03
EMA Stack — EMA21 > EMA55 > EMA200
Enter the three EMA values. All must be in descending order (price > EMA21 > EMA55 > EMA200) for a full bull stack. The EMA21/55 gap tells trend maturity. 1–3% gap = fresh trend (best). The trend maturity grade (S++ to X) determines your position size.
04
RSI14 + ADX — Momentum Dual Confirmation
RSI must be between 50–70 (sweet spot). ADX must be above 28. Both conditions required. RSI 50–70 = trend momentum without being overbought. ADX >28 = directional move (not ranging). These two together confirm a genuine trend, not just noise.
05
Fibonacci — Where is price in the swing?
Enter your swing high and swing low. Ideal entry: price pulling back to 38.2% or 61.8% Fibonacci support in a bull trend. The calculator will highlight the nearest level. A bounce from 38.2%/61.8% with RSI >50 and ADX >28 is the gold standard gold entry.
06
Execute — Enter with ATR-Based Stop Loss
Gold uses ATR × 1.8 for stops (wider than stocks due to volatility). Never use a fixed pip stop for gold. Enter on the candle that closes above EMA21 after a pullback. Set stop immediately. Risk maximum 1% of account. Use the Kelly calculator to determine exact position size in oz or lots.
◈ Gold Key Levels & Patterns
🎯
Round Numbers Matter: $2000, $2100, $2200, $2300 etc. are psychological levels where gold pauses. Expect resistance at round numbers and always target levels just before them (e.g., TP at $2295, not $2300).
📅
NFP Day (1st Friday of month): Extreme volatility. Do not hold gold positions into NFP release. Close before 12:30 UTC or accept very wide stops.
🏦
Fed Decisions: Hawkish Fed (rate hikes) = bearish gold. Dovish Fed (rate cuts) = very bullish gold. Avoid new entries 24h before Fed meetings.
🌍
Geopolitical Spikes: Gold spikes 1–3% on war/crisis news. These are momentum trades — enter after the first pullback, not on the initial spike (spreads are terrible on spike candles).
🕐
Best Entry Times: 08:30 UTC (London momentum kick-off) and 13:30 UTC (NY open — highest volume). Avoid 00:00–07:00 UTC (low volume Asia session).
📊
Position Sizing for Gold: Gold moves $15–40 per day (ATR). At 1 troy oz position with $20 ATR, your 1× ATR stop = $20 per oz. With $10,000 account at 1% risk = max 5 oz position. The risk calculator in the Gold tab does this math for you.
⚖️ Professional Risk Management — The Rules That Protect Your Capital
Risk management is the difference between consistent profitability and account blow-up. A trader with a 45% win rate but 3:1 reward-to-risk is far more profitable than one with 65% win rate and 1:1 RR. Master these rules before you trade live.
Rule 1
The 1% Rule — Maximum Risk Per Trade
Never risk more than 1% of your total account on a single trade. With a $10,000 account, max loss per trade = $100. This allows 100 losing trades in a row before you're wiped out — mentally and mathematically sustainable. Enter your Account Size and Risk % in the calculator to get exact position sizing.
Rule 2
ATR-Based Stop Loss — Never Fixed Pips
Use ATR × 1.5 for stocks, ATR × 1.8 for gold. ATR adapts to current volatility — tight in calm markets, wider in volatile ones. A fixed 50-pip stop gets triggered randomly. An ATR stop lets the trade breathe naturally. Enter ATR14 in the calculator and use the generated Trade Plan.
Rule 3
Minimum 1.5:1 Reward-to-Risk Ratio
Only take trades where TP1 ≥ 1.5× your stop distance. The calculator's TP1 is set at ATR×1.5 (1:1 RR). TP2 is 3× ATR (2:1 RR). TP3 is 5× ATR (3.3:1 RR). Splitting your position across TP1/TP2/TP3 achieves an average RR >2:1 even on partial wins.
Rule 4
Breakeven Stop After TP1
The moment TP1 is hit (taking 40% of position off), immediately move your Stop Loss to your entry price (breakeven). This makes the remaining trade a "free" trade — it can only make money from this point. This single rule dramatically improves profitability and reduces emotional pressure.
Rule 5
Maximum 3 Open Positions (Correlated Assets)
Don't hold 5 tech stocks simultaneously — they're 80% correlated. If the sector sells off, all 5 stop out at once. Diversify across sectors or asset classes. Gold + Tech + Commodity is better than 3 tech stocks. Each open position should ideally have <0.5 correlation with others.
Rule 6
Daily Loss Limit — Stop Trading After -3%
If you lose 3% of account in a single day, stop trading. Log out. Return tomorrow with fresh eyes. Revenge trading after a loss is where accounts die. The market will be there tomorrow — your capital may not be if you keep trading emotionally.
Rule 7
Half-Kelly Position Sizing
Kelly Criterion calculates the mathematically optimal position size. But full Kelly is too aggressive for real markets. Use Half-Kelly (shown in the trade plan). With a 55% win rate and 1.8 RR, full Kelly ≈ 25% of account — far too risky. Half-Kelly ≈ 12.5%, which compounds well while surviving drawdowns.
Rule 8
Only Trade When Score ≥ 70/100
The calculator's score reflects filter confluence. Score 70+ = at least 5 of 7 major filters aligned. Score 50–70 = marginal setup, reduce size by 50%. Score <50 = do not trade regardless of "feeling" or market FOMO. Patience between high-probability setups is a skill — practice it.

📊 Indicators Reference — What Each Value Means
📈 MA / EMA Values
Market PriceThe current live price of the asset. Use the last traded price or bid price from your broker platform.
MA5 / EMA8Fast average. Represents immediate 5–8 day momentum. Found in any charting platform under "MA(5)" or "EMA(8)". Use closing prices.
MA20 / EMA21The monthly average (~20 trading days). Core reference for intermediate momentum. This is the most important MA level.
MA50 / EMA55Quarterly average (~50 days). Institutional buying/selling level. Used by most fund managers as a key decision level.
MA200 / EMA200Annual average. The most important macro trend indicator. Above = bull market. Below = bear market. Respected by all major institutions.
📉 KDJ Oscillator
K valueFast stochastic line (typically 9-period). Range 0–100. Similar to %K in traditional stochastics. Above 50 = bullish momentum.
D valueSlow signal line (3-period MA of K). K crossing above D = buy signal. K crossing below D = sell signal.
J valueThe most sensitive line: J = 3K − 2D. Range can exceed 0–100. J<20 = oversold bounce signal. J>80 = overbought, reduce size. J>100 = extreme overbought — exit.
📊 MACD Values
DIF (MACD Line)The difference between EMA12 and EMA26. Also called the "MACD line" or "fast line". Positive = short-term average above long-term = bullish. Find as "MACD" in most platforms.
DEA (Signal Line)9-period EMA of the DIF. Also called "Signal" or "DEA". DIF crossing above DEA = bullish crossover. Find as "Signal" in MACD indicator settings.
Histogram (MACD Bar)The bar chart shown under MACD. = DIF − DEA. Growing bars = momentum building. Shrinking bars = momentum fading (possible reversal warning). Use for timing: enter when histogram turns from negative to positive.
⚡ ADX (Average Directional Index)
ADX14Measures trend strength (0–100), NOT direction. <20 = no trend (ranging). 20–28 = developing. 28–50 = strong trend. >50 = very strong/unusual. Find in platform as "ADX(14)" or "Average Directional Movement".
How to useMA/EMA signals are only reliable when ADX >20. In ranging markets (ADX <20), moving averages give false crossover signals. Always check ADX before acting on any MA crossover.
🎯 ATR (Average True Range)
ATR14The average daily price range over 14 periods. Shows current volatility. Higher ATR = wider price swings = need wider stops. For stocks: ATR14 might be $0.02–$0.50. For gold: ATR14 typically $15–$40 per troy oz.
How to findIn TradingView: search "ATR" indicator, use 14-period. In MetaTrader: Average True Range(14). The current value shown at the bottom of the indicator panel is what you enter here.
Stop formulaStop Loss = Entry Price − (ATR14 × 1.5). For gold use × 1.8. This is calculated automatically in the Trade Plan card once you enter ATR14 and Market Price.
📏 RSI (Relative Strength Index)
RSI14Momentum oscillator 0–100. For gold, ideal zone is 50–70. <30 = oversold (potential bounce). >70 = overbought (potential reversal). Find in any charting platform as "RSI(14)".
The 50 RuleRSI above 50 = net bullish momentum. RSI below 50 = net bearish momentum. Use 50 as the bull/bear dividing line. Best entries: RSI just crossed above 50 and heading toward 65.
☁️ Ichimoku Cloud (EMA tab)
Above CloudPrice is above the Kumo (cloud). Strong bullish signal — institutional buyers in control. The cloud itself acts as support. Bullish confirmation.
Inside CloudPrice is inside the cloud. Indecision zone. Wait for clear breakout above cloud before entering long. Avoid trading inside the cloud.
Below CloudPrice below cloud. Bearish — do not buy. The cloud acts as overhead resistance. Only acceptable in very short-term momentum scalps if all other indicators are extremely bullish.
📊 Volume Ratio
How to calculateVolume Ratio = Today's Volume ÷ 20-Day Average Volume. E.g., if today volume = 2M and avg = 1M, ratio = 2.0. Many platforms show this directly as "Relative Volume" or "Vol/Avg".
Interpretation>1.5× = strong conviction move. 1.0–1.5× = normal. <0.8× = low conviction — avoid breakout entries. Never buy a breakout on below-average volume — it will likely fail.
