∿ MA vs EMA — Which to Use?
Both MA and EMA track price trends, but EMA reacts faster by weighting recent candles more heavily. Use MA for swing trades on Bursa (less noise, cleaner signals). Use EMA for US intraday/momentum trading where speed matters. The 7-filter system is identical in logic — only the averages differ.
∿ MA Stack (5/20/50/200)
✅ Better for Bursa Malaysia stocks
✅ Smoother — fewer whipsaws
✅ Best on Daily/Weekly timeframes
⚠️ Slower to react to reversals
📐 F1 Ideal: price ≤2% above MA20
≋ EMA Stack (8/21/55/200)
✅ Better for US stocks & Gold
✅ Faster — catches momentum early
✅ Best on 15m/1H/4H/Daily
⚠️ More whipsaws in choppy markets
📐 F1 Ideal: price ≤2% above EMA8
∿ 7-Filter Entry Checklist
F1
Price above MA5 / EMA8 — Immediate Momentum
The fastest filter. Price must close above the fast average. Distance matters: 0–2% = ideal (full size), 2–5% = acceptable (enter), 5–10% = stretched (50% size), >10% = overextended (skip, wait for pullback).
🎯 Best entry: price dips to touch MA5/EMA8 on low volume then bounces
F2
MA5>MA20 / EMA8>EMA21 — Short Trend Alignment
The fast average must be above the medium average. This confirms the short-term trend direction. When F2 just crossed (gap 0–2%), you are in the highest-probability entry window — the trend is fresh.
🚀 Just crossed (0–2% gap) = best risk/reward entry
F3
MA20>MA50 / EMA21>EMA55 — Trend Maturity Grade
Determines trend stage. Grade S++ (1–3%) = institutional trend just starting. Grade A (3–5%) = healthy. Grade B/C (5–10%) = maturing, reduce size. Grade X (>10%) = exhausted — do not start new positions.
⚠️ Grade X overrides PROCEED → auto-downgrades to WATCH
F4
Price above MA200 / EMA200 — Macro Trend
The annual average. Above = bull market regime. Below = bear market. Large funds use EMA200 as their buy/sell dividing line. Even if F1/F2/F3 pass, fighting the EMA200 cuts your win rate significantly.
🌐 Optional but highly recommended — adds 8 points to score
F5
KDJ Oscillator — Momentum Timing
K>D = bullish. J<20 = oversold bounce (best reversal entry signal). J>80 = overbought (reduce size or wait). J>100 = extreme — exit. The J line is most sensitive and gives the earliest signal.
💡 J<20 turning up while K>D = highest-conviction bounce entry
F6
MACD DIF>DEA — Momentum Direction
DIF above DEA = bullish. Both above zero = strong bull. MACD histogram growing = accelerating momentum. Histogram shrinking = momentum fading (prepare to exit). The crossover moment (DIF just crossed above DEA) is a high-probability entry signal.
🚀 DIF just crossed above DEA = strongest MACD entry signal
F7
Volume Ratio >1.5× — Conviction Confirmation
Volume must be at least 1.5× the 20-day average on breakout days. Low volume breakouts fail 70%+ of the time. High volume breakouts have institutional participation. Never chase a breakout on below-average volume.
📊 Volume ratio >2× on breakout = institutional accumulation confirmed
∿ Trade Setups — When to Enter
🚀 Setup A — Fresh Breakout
EMA8 just crossed EMA21 (F2 gap 0–2%), price 0–2% above EMA8, ADX rising above 25, MACD histogram turning positive. Highest win rate. Full position. Stop = 1×ATR below EMA8.
📈 Setup B — EMA8 Pullback
Trend established (EMA8>21>55), price pulls back to touch EMA8 on low volume, KDJ J rising from <30. Best risk/reward. Full position. SL = EMA8 − ATR×1.5.
⚠️ Setup C — Momentum Chase
All EMAs aligned but price 5–10% above EMA8. If ADX>40 and volume surging, enter 25% size only. Mandatory tight stop. Exit any candle close below EMA8 immediately.
🔴 Never Trade
EMA21 below EMA55, price below EMA8, ADX<20 (ranging), K<D, DIF<DEA, or price below EMA200. These setups have <40% win rate regardless of other signals.
◈ Gold XAUUSD — Unique Drivers
Gold is driven by 4 forces: USD strength (inverse ~80%), real interest rates, geopolitical risk, and central bank demand. Always check DXY before entering. Use ATR×1.8 for stops — gold moves $15–40/day and needs wider room.
💵
DXY (Dollar Index)
DXY falling = gold bullish. DXY rising = gold bearish. This relationship holds ~80% of the time. A falling DXY adds 5 bonus points to your gold score. Always check before entry.
📊
RSI Sweet Spot 50–70
Gold's ideal entry RSI is 50–70. Below 50 = momentum not confirmed. Above 70 = overbought (reduce size). Above 80 = extreme — hold existing only. RSI 50 is the most important bull/bear dividing line for gold.
⏰
London-NY Overlap (Prime)
9:00 PM – 1:00 AM MYT. 60% of daily gold volume trades in this window. Best breakouts happen here. Avoid new entries in Asian session (1–4 PM MYT) — spreads widen and moves are thin.
📐
Fibonacci Levels
Gold respects Fib levels strongly due to algo trading. 38.2% and 61.8% are key pullback supports in a bull trend. Enter at 38.2% if trend is strong. Enter at 61.8% if you missed 38.2%. Below 78.6% warns of reversal.
📅
News Events to Avoid
NFP (1st Friday of month): Close before 8:30 PM MYT. FOMC meetings: Avoid 24h before. Hawkish Fed = bearish gold. Dovish Fed = very bullish gold. CPI data: High inflation = gold positive.
🎯
Round Numbers
$2000, $2100, $2200 etc. are psychological resistance levels. Always set TP just below round numbers (e.g., TP at $2295 not $2300). Institutions take profits at round numbers creating predictable resistance.
◈ Gold 6-Step Entry Protocol
01
Macro — EMA200 + DXY check
Is price above EMA200? Is DXY flat or falling? If NO to both = do not trade long today. Takes 30 seconds but prevents costly counter-trend losses.
02
Session — Are you in London or NY hours?
Check the session badge top-right. London-NY overlap = green light for full size. Outside prime = 50% size or wait. Thin sessions create whipsaws that trigger stops unfairly.
03
EMA Stack — EMA21 > EMA55 > EMA200
All three in bull order. The EMA21/55 gap gives trend grade (S++ to X). Fresh 1–3% gap = best entry. Enter the values in the Gold tab and check the grade badge.
04
RSI 50–70 + ADX >28
Both required simultaneously. RSI 50–70 = trending without overbought. ADX>28 = genuine directional move. These two together filter out ranging periods that fool single-indicator traders.
05
Fibonacci — Find the pullback level
Enter swing high and low. Look for price at 38.2% or 61.8% in a bull trend. A bounce from these levels with RSI>50 and ADX>28 is the gold standard entry combination.
06
Execute — ATR×1.8 stop, 1% account risk
Enter on candle that closes above EMA21 after pullback. Set stop immediately at ATR×1.8 below entry. Risk max 1% of account. Use the Kelly block in the trade plan for exact position size in oz.

🔄 Swing Reversal — V-Bottom Detection
A swing reversal trade captures the turn from a downtrend to an uptrend. The key is catching the V-Bottom candle — a single candle that marks the exhaustion of sellers and the first sign of buyers taking control. This requires multiple confirming signals before entering.
S1
Reversal Candle Pattern
Hammer: Long lower wick (≥2× body), small body at top, minimal upper wick. Sellers pushed price down hard but buyers reclaimed by close. Bullish Engulfing: Green candle that fully engulfs the previous red candle body — buyers completely overwhelmed sellers. Doji at low: Open = Close near the bottom — complete indecision after a downtrend = potential reversal.
🎯 Hammer at support + high volume = highest probability reversal signal
S2
Supertrend Flip — Trend Change Confirmation
The Supertrend indicator flipping from red (dots above price) to green (dots below price) is the strongest mechanical reversal signal. Enter when: previous Supertrend was above price AND current Supertrend is below price. This combination with a reversal candle = very high conviction.
🟢 "Just Flipped Bullish" = strongest Supertrend signal — enter immediately
S3
KDJ Oversold Bounce
J value below 20 = oversold. J turning up from below 20 while K crosses above D = classic reversal timing signal. This tells you buyers are starting to regain control after the selloff. The lower the J dipped (negative J), the stronger the bounce potential.
💡 J<20 + K crossing above D = buy signal
S4
MA Rebuild — Trend Recovery
After a reversal candle, watch for price to rebuild above MA5, then MA20, then MA50 in sequence. Each successful reclaim adds conviction. Enter on the first close above MA5. Add on MA20 reclaim. This is how you build a position gradually as the reversal confirms.
📈 Enter at MA5 reclaim · Add at MA20 · Full size at MA50 reclaim
S5
Multi-Level Targets (TP1/2/3)
Swing trades use 3 take-profit levels: TP1 = ATR×1.5 (take 40%), TP2 = nearest resistance (take 35%), TP3 = swing high from before the reversal (take 25%). Move stop to breakeven immediately when TP1 hits. Trail stop under each MA as price climbs.
⚖️ Partial exits protect profit while letting runners run
🔄 Swing Reversal Scoring System
⚡
Score 80–100: All signals aligned. Full position entry. High conviction V-bottom. Rare but highly profitable.
✅
Score 60–79: Good reversal setup. 75% position. Most standard reversal trades fall here.
⚠️
Score 40–59: Partial signals. 25–50% position only. Wait for one more confirming signal before adding.
🔴
Score <40: Do not enter. Dead-cat bounce risk is high. Wait for full setup confirmation.
📅
Timeframe Matters: Daily reversal candles are far more reliable than 15m or 1H. A daily hammer at major support is a high-conviction trade; a 15m hammer is noise.
🛑
Reversal Failure: If price closes below the low of the reversal candle — exit immediately. The reversal has failed. No averaging down.

⏱ Timeframe Selection Guide
1m
1-Minute — Scalping Only
Hold 5–30 minutes. F1 ideal ≤0.8%. ADX minimum 18. Use only during Prime window open (first 30 min). Extremely noisy — one bad tick can trigger your stop. Only for experienced scalpers who can watch the screen continuously.
🎯 Prime window only · 9:30–10:00 PM MYT (EDT)
5m
5-Minute — Intraday Core Timeframe
Hold 30 min – 2 hours. F1 ideal ≤1.5%. ADX minimum 20. The workhorse of intraday trading. Use 15m for trend direction, 5m for setup, 1m for entry timing. Best during Prime and Power Hour windows.
✅ Open & Power Hour · Most traders' primary intraday chart
15m
15-Minute — MTF Bias Chart
Hold 1–4 hours. F1 ideal ≤2%. ADX minimum 20. Use this for trend direction before entering on 5m. Never trade against the 15m trend. Also good standalone for traders who cannot watch screens constantly.
📊 Check 15m trend BEFORE entering on 5m chart
1H/4H
1-Hour / 4-Hour — Swing Setup
Hold 4 hours to 3 days. F1 ideal ≤2.5–3%. ADX minimum 22–25. Session-independent — can be traded any time. Use 1H for entries during lull periods. 4H is ideal for Bursa ETF swing trades where you hold 1–5 days.
🇲🇾 Best for Bursa ETF + US swing trades
Daily
Daily — Position Trading
Hold days to 2 weeks. F1 ideal ≤3.5%. ADX minimum 25. The default for swing trading. Signals are highly reliable — one false breakout doesn't matter because daily closes filter most noise. Best for part-time traders who check charts once a day.
⭐ Default for most traders — signals are cleaner and more reliable
Weekly
Weekly — Long-Term Position
Hold weeks to months. F1 ideal ≤5%. ADX minimum 28. Use weekly MA200 as the ultimate macro filter. Weekly signals are extremely reliable but rare. Suitable for investors who check once a week. ATR×1.8 for stops on weekly charts.
🏦 For long-term gold ETF and index positions
⏱ Multi-Timeframe (MTF) Trading Rule
📐
The Golden Rule: Higher TF = trend direction. Lower TF = entry timing. Never use a 5m chart to determine trend direction — use 15m or 1H for that, then drop to 5m to find the entry candle.
✅
Full Alignment: Daily bullish + 4H bullish + 1H bullish = trade in the direction of all three. This is the highest conviction scenario — use full position size.
⚠️
Conflict: Daily bullish but 1H bearish = wait for 1H to turn bullish before entering. Never trade against the Daily trend on lower timeframes.
🔥
Use the MTF Tab: Enter RSI, ADX, MACD, EMA alignment across 15m/1H/4H/Daily. The heatmap shows instantly where momentum is aligned or conflicting.
📊 Support & Resistance Zone Analyzer — How to Use
Support and resistance levels are price zones where buying or selling pressure has historically reversed price. The S/R Zone Analyzer identifies these levels, calculates their strength through confluence, and detects potential reversal zones automatically.
01
Import MA/EMA Levels
Click "⬇ Import MA Levels" or "⬇ Import EMA Levels" to pull your current MA/EMA values directly from those tabs. Your MA5/20/50/200 (or EMA8/21/55/200) automatically become S/R levels. This is the fastest way to populate the chart.
💡 Import first, then add manual levels (previous swing highs/lows, round numbers)
02
Add Manual Key Levels
Add your own levels: previous day's high/low, weekly high/low, round numbers ($1.50, $2.00), recent swing highs and lows, earnings gap levels. Label them clearly. The more levels you add, the better the confluence detection works.
📌 Include at minimum: prev high, prev low, nearest round number
03
Add Pivot Points
Enter yesterday's High, Low, and Close. The calculator generates 7 pivot levels (P, R1, R2, R3, S1, S2, S3) automatically. Pivot levels are used by institutional traders — when multiple pivots cluster near your MA levels, that's a high-strength zone.
🎯 Pivot + MA confluence = institutional-grade support/resistance
04
Read the Confluence Zones
Any zone where 2+ levels are within 1.5% of each other = Confluence Zone (shown in yellow/amber). 3+ levels = Major Zone (shown in gold with thick band). These are the most powerful S/R levels because multiple analytical methods agree.
⭐ Major Zones = highest probability reversal or bounce areas
05
Use the R:R Ratio
The analysis card shows Risk:Reward = distance to nearest resistance ÷ distance to nearest support. R:R ≥ 2:1 = take the trade. R:R 1.2–2:1 = acceptable. R:R <1.2 = skip (you're too close to resistance). Always check R:R before entering.
⚖️ Minimum 1.5:1 R:R before entering any trade
📊 Reversal Zone Detection
🔄
Bullish Reversal Zone: Price approaching a Major Confluence Support (3+ levels within 1.5%) from above. Look for reversal candles (hammer, engulfing) on that zone with oversold KDJ. High-probability long entry.
📉
Bearish Reversal Zone: Price approaching a Major Confluence Resistance from below. Look for bearish engulfing or shooting star candles. Reduce long positions or set tight trailing stops.
💡
Zone Width Matters: A zone that has been tested 3+ times (bounced or rejected) is stronger than a fresh level. The more times a zone has been tested without breaking, the more orders are stacked there.
⚡
Distance Bar Chart: The bar chart at the bottom shows all levels as % distance from current price. Levels within 2% of price are your immediate targets/stops. Use these to set limit orders and stop losses precisely.
⚖️ The 8 Risk Rules That Protect Your Capital
Risk management is the difference between consistent profitability and account blow-up. A trader with 45% win rate but 3:1 RR is far more profitable than one with 65% win rate and 1:1 RR. Master these before trading live.
Rule 1
1% Rule — Max Risk Per Trade
Never risk more than 1% of account on a single trade. $10,000 account = $100 max loss per trade. This allows 100 consecutive losses before wipeout — mathematically and mentally sustainable. Beginners: 0.5%.
Rule 2
ATR Stop — Never Fixed Pips
Stop = ATR×1.5 for stocks, ATR×1.8 for gold. ATR adapts to current volatility. Fixed pip stops get triggered randomly. ATR stops let trades breathe naturally while cutting losers at a statistically meaningful level.
Rule 3
Minimum 1.5:1 Reward:Risk
Only take trades where TP1 ≥ 1.5× stop distance. TP1 at ATR×1.5 = 1:1. TP2 at ATR×3 = 2:1. TP3 at ATR×5 = 3.3:1. Split position across all three = average RR >2:1 even on partial wins.
Rule 4
Breakeven After TP1
When TP1 hits (take 40% off), move stop to entry price immediately. The remaining position is now a "free trade." This single rule dramatically improves long-term profitability by eliminating winners turning into losers.
Rule 5
Max 3 Correlated Positions
5 tech stocks = 80% correlation. If sector sells off, all 5 stop out simultaneously. Diversify: Gold + Tech + Commodity. Each position should have <0.5 correlation with others to avoid portfolio-wide stop-outs.
Rule 6
Daily Loss Limit −3%
If you lose 3% of account in one day, close the app and stop trading. Return tomorrow. Revenge trading after a loss is where accounts go to die. The market will be there tomorrow — your capital may not be.
Rule 7
Half-Kelly Position Sizing
Use Half-Kelly (shown in trade plan). Full Kelly is mathematically optimal but too aggressive for real markets. With 55% win rate and 1.8 RR: Full Kelly ≈ 25% of account. Half-Kelly ≈ 12.5%. Compounds well while surviving drawdowns.
Rule 8
Only Trade Score ≥ 70/100
Score 70+ = 5 of 7 major filters aligned. Score 50–70 = reduce size 50%. Score <50 = do not trade regardless of FOMO or "gut feel." Patience between high-probability setups is a skill that separates profitable traders from gamblers.
🔬 Indicators Reference — What Each Value Means & How to Find It
📈 MA / EMA
MA5 / EMA8Fast average (5–8 days). Immediate momentum. In TradingView: type "MA" → set period 5. In platform: "Moving Average (5)".
MA20 / EMA21Monthly average (~20 trading days). Most important MA level. Core reference for intermediate momentum. Set period 20 or 21.
MA50 / EMA55Quarterly average. Institutional buying level. Fund managers use this as key decision level. Set period 50 or 55.
MA200 / EMA200Annual average. The macro trend indicator. Above = bull market. Below = bear market. All major institutions respect this level.
📉 KDJ Oscillator
K valueFast stochastic (9-period). Range 0–100. Above 50 = bullish momentum. In TradingView: search "KDJ". In many platforms: "Stoch RSI".
D valueSlow signal line (3-period MA of K). K crossing above D = buy signal. K crossing below D = sell signal.
J valueMost sensitive: J = 3K − 2D. Range exceeds 0–100. J<20 = oversold bounce. J>80 = overbought reduce size. J>100 = extreme overbought exit.
📊 MACD
DIF (MACD line)EMA12 − EMA26. Positive = short-term above long-term = bullish. In TradingView: search "MACD", take the "MACD" value (not signal).
DEA (Signal line)9-period EMA of DIF. Also called "Signal". DIF crossing above DEA = bullish crossover. In TradingView take "Signal" value.
HistogramDIF − DEA. Growing bars = momentum building. Shrinking = fading. Enter when histogram turns from negative to positive.
⚡ ADX
ADX14Trend strength 0–100. NOT direction. <20 = no trend (ranging). 20–28 = developing. 28–50 = strong. >50 = very strong. In TradingView: "ADX DI" indicator.
How to useMA/EMA signals only reliable when ADX >20. In ranging markets (ADX<20) EMAs give false crossovers. Always check ADX before acting on any crossover signal.
🎯 ATR
ATR14Average daily price range over 14 periods. Current volatility measure. Stocks: $0.02–$0.50. Gold: $15–40/oz. In TradingView: search "ATR", use 14-period default.
Stop formulaSL = Entry − (ATR×1.5) for stocks. SL = Entry − (ATR×1.8) for gold. Calculated automatically in Trade Plan card once ATR and Market Price are entered.
📏 RSI
RSI14Momentum oscillator 0–100. Stocks ideal zone: 50–72. Gold ideal: 50–70. <30 = oversold. >70 = overbought. In TradingView: search "RSI", default is 14-period.
The 50 RuleRSI above 50 = net bullish momentum. RSI below 50 = net bearish. Best entries: RSI just crossed above 50 heading toward 65. The 50 line is the most important RSI level.
☁️ Ichimoku Cloud
Above CloudPrice above Kumo = strong bullish. Institutional buyers in control. Cloud acts as support. In TradingView: "Ichimoku Cloud" — look at where price is relative to the shaded area.
Inside CloudIndecision zone. Wait for clear breakout above before entering. The cloud width shows strength — thin cloud = weak support/resistance.
Below CloudBearish — do not buy. Cloud acts as overhead resistance. Only acceptable in very short-term scalps if all other indicators are extremely bullish.
📊 Volume & VWAP
Volume RatioToday's volume ÷ 20-day average. >1.5× = conviction move. 1.0–1.5× = normal. <0.8× = avoid breakout entries. In TradingView: "Relative Volume" indicator.
VWAPVolume-Weighted Average Price. Institutional benchmark for the day. Price above VWAP = intraday bulls in control. Best intraday entry: first pullback to VWAP in a bullish trend. In TradingView: search "VWAP".
🔄 Supertrend
What it isA trend-following indicator that plots a line above (bearish) or below (bullish) price. Based on ATR. In TradingView: search "Supertrend", use default settings (3, 10) or (3, 7).
Flip signalWhen Supertrend flips from above to below price (red to green) = strong buy signal. Especially powerful when it coincides with price closing above a key MA. The flip candle is your entry candle.

03
📊 Put/Call Ratio — CBOE Daily Figure
Get the CBOE Total P/C Ratio from cboe.com/us/options/market_statistics/daily or search Google for "CBOE put call ratio today". Published after US market close each day. Typical range: 0.60–1.30.

This measures how many put options (bets on falling prices) vs call options (bets on rising prices) traders are buying. Uses it as a contrarian signal — extreme put buying means extreme fear, which often precedes a bounce.

>1.2 → extreme fear in options market → contrarian BUY signal, adds +1 to Bias Score.
0.9–1.2 → bearish sentiment → proceed with caution, no bonus.
0.7–0.9 → neutral → normal trading.
<0.7 → complacency / everyone bullish → market may be overbought, deducts −1.
💡 Update once per day — use previous day's closing figure

06
📊 Volume Profile Zones (S/R Tab) — Order Flow Context
Volume Profile shows where most trading volume occurred at each price level. In TradingView: click the Volume Profile tool in the left toolbar (looks like a sideways histogram) → drag across the last 20–60 days → it draws automatically.

POC (Point of Control): The price level with the highest volume — the fairest value according to the market. Price is magnetically attracted to the POC. Great level for limit orders.
HVN (High Volume Node): Tall bars in the profile = price spent a lot of time here = strong support/resistance. If price approaches an HVN from above, expect it to slow down or bounce. Enter the HVN prices in the S/R Zone tab — they will show as thick bands on the chart.
LVN (Low Volume Node): Thin/small bars = very little trading happened here = price moves fast through this zone. LVNs between your entry and target are good — price will accelerate through them. LVNs between entry and stop are bad — your stop could trigger quickly.
💡 Add POC + 2 HVNs to the S/R Zone tab every day — these are key institutional reference levels
